**Propositional Logic** — simple True/False facts, no variables.

- `P = "It is raining"` — that's it, one fixed fact
- Cannot say "all humans are mortal" — only individual facts

**First-Order Logic (FOL)** — adds variables, predicates, and quantifiers.

- `∀x: Human(x) → Mortal(x)` — works for *every* human at once
- Far more expressive, used in real AI systems

> Think of it this way: Propositional Logic is a list of on/off switches. FOL is a full rulebook that applies to any object you throw at it.
